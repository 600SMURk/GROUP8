WASTE TO ENERGY.

As our name goes, waste to energy is basically the process of transforming non- recyclable waste into usable forms of energy, including electricity and heat. This would be a very good initiative, since it would be dealing with two of the most important sectors of our country. That is the power and waste management sectors.


As we all know, improper waste management is a conquer to a developing country like us, therefore the motive for this project is to shift from disposing of waste to managing it in a way that minimizes its environmental impact and maximizes resource recovery.


Looking at the population growth rate of the country, with about 34 million people since 2023 and the amount of waste disposed by each individual per day. Research shows that about 12,000 tonnes of solid waste is disposed daily in the country, which is about 4,380,000 tonnes per year. These waste have a wide range of detrimental effects on the environment, impacting various aspects of ecosystems and human health. Water pollution, Air pollution and spread of diseases are few effects of these waste to life. Hence, giving significant attention to waste in the environment is crucial since it is impacting both our immediate well-being and the long-term health of the planet.


Waste to Energy solutions Ghana, our focus is to produce renewable electricity from these waste to the people of our country through a process called incineration.This would not give us only renewable energy but also, it will ensure proper disposal methods of waste in the country, particularly the urban areas.

In this process, tonnes of waste is burned in a controlled environment, releasing a significant amount of heat.This heat is use to boil water, creating high-pressure steam. The high-pressure steam is directed at a turbine, causing it to spin. The spinning turbine powers an electrical generator, which converts the mechanical energy into a greater megawatts of electricity.


This electricity would have the potential of replacing our recent power crisis popularly known as "DUMSOR". Another essential material produced during this process is the ashe, which seems useless but trust us, it is useful in the production of cement.


Essentially, the incineration process acts as a heat source, much like burning fossil fuels in a traditional power plant. The key difference is that the fuel is municipal solid waste, which does not produce any harmful pollutants like the petrol and diesel does. Our modern waste-to-energy incineration facility would be designed to maximize the efficiency of this process, recovering as much energy as possible from the waste. It is very important to also understand that the facility would have very advanced air polution control systems. These systems would be designed to remove as much polution from the gasses created by the burning process as possible and so no air pollution. This shows that this process would be eco-friendly and harmless to us as humans and our environment at large.
